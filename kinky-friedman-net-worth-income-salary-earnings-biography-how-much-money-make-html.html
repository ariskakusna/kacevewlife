    Richard Samet “Kinky” Friedman (born November 1, 1944) is an American country music singer, songwriter, novelist, humorist, politician, and former columnist for Texas Monthly. He considers himself to be a contemporary of popular American satirists Will Rogers and Mark Twain, and his music is influenced by their works. In the 2006 election for the post of Governor of Texas, he was one of two independent candidates running for the position. A total of 12.6 percent of votes were cast for Friedman, who finished fourth in the six-person contest.
