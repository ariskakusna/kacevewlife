    Goosebumps Season 2:- Goosebumps, the popular horror book series by R.L. Stine, has been adapted into a new TV show on Disney+ and Hulu. The first season of the show, which premiered on October 13, 2023, consists of 10 episodes that feature different stories inspired by the books, such as Say Cheese and Die!, Go Eat Worms!, and The Cuckoo Clock of Doom. The show also has an overarching plot that involves a mysterious spirit named Harold Biddle, who possesses an English teacher named Mr. Bratt and causes havoc in the town of Port Lawrence.

    The show has received positive reviews from critics and fans, who praised the cast, the humor, the nostalgia, and the balance between horror and comedy. The show has also been compared to other anthologies like Black Mirror and The Twilight Zone, as well as the original Goosebumps TV series from the 1990s. Many viewers are eager to know if there will be a second season of the show, and what stories it will feature. Here is everything you need to know about Goosebumps Season 2.

    Is Goosebumps Season 2 Confirmed?

    As of November 22, 2023, Goosebumps Season 2 has not been officially confirmed by Disney. However, the producers and the creators of the show have expressed their hope and interest in making more seasons and episodes. In an exclusive interview with The Direct, producers Rob Letterman and Nicholas Stoller said that they wish they could greenlight everything they wanted, and that they loved making the show and working with everyone involved. They also said that they have a lot of story to tell, and that they have a lot of hooks in there to tee up if they get lucky enough to do another season.

    Similarly, in an interview with Comicbook.com, producer Conor Welch said that their hope is to follow this group of kids and adults for many, many more seasons and many, many more episodes to come. He also said that they have every one of the books of the canon at their disposal, and that there is just a lot more to dig into.

    The chances of renewal depend on the ratings and the popularity of the show, as well as the availability of the cast and the crew. Since the show is a joint venture between Disney+ and Hulu, it may also depend on the streaming platforms’ strategies and budgets. However, given the positive reception and the fan demand, it seems likely that Goosebumps Season 2 will be announced soon.

    Release Date

    If Goosebumps Season 2 is confirmed, the release date will depend on the production schedule and the number of episodes. The first season of the show was filmed in Atlanta, Georgia, from February to June 2023. Assuming that the second season will follow a similar timeline, and that there will be no delays or complications due to the COVID-19 pandemic or other factors, the earliest possible release date for Goosebumps Season 2 could be October 2024, just in time for Halloween.

    Time Zones

    However, this is only a prediction, and the actual release date may vary depending on various factors. The show may also opt for a different release strategy, such as releasing the episodes weekly or in batches, instead of dropping the entire season at once. The first season of the show released the first five episodes on October 13, 2023, and the remaining five episodes on October 27, 2023. The second season may follow a similar pattern, or change it up depending on the feedback and the preferences of the viewers.

    What Will Goosebumps Season 2 Be About?

    Goosebumps Season 2 will most likely continue the stories of the main characters from the first season, as well as introduce new stories based on the books. The first season of the show ended with a cliffhanger, as Harold Biddle escaped from Mr. Bratt’s body and possessed Lucas, one of the kids who had been investigating the strange events in Port Lawrence. The second season will probably explore the consequences of this twist, and how the other kids will try to stop Harold and save Lucas.

    The second season will also feature new stories inspired by the books, as the producers and the creators have said that they have many more stories to draw from. R.L. Stine has written over 200 books in the Goosebumps series, and there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the stories that were considered for the first season, but did not make the cut, include The Girl Who Cried Monster, The Haunted School, and The Horror at Camp Jellyjam. These stories may be revisited for the second season, or new ones may be selected.

    Spoiler Alert

    Night of the Living Dummy: This story features Slappy, the evil ventriloquist dummy who is one of the most iconic and recurring villains in the Goosebumps series. Slappy has appeared in several books, as well as in the Goosebumps movies starring Jack Black. Slappy is also hinted at in the first season of the show, as one of the items in Mr. Bratt’s classroom. The second season could bring Slappy to life, and have him terrorize the kids and the town.

    Welcome to Dead House: This story is the first book in the original Goosebumps series, and it tells the tale of a family that moves into a haunted house that is surrounded by zombies. The second season could adapt this story, and add some twists and surprises to make it more relevant and exciting for the modern audience.

    The Werewolf of Fever Swamp: This story is about a boy who moves to a swampy area with his family, and encounters a mysterious creature that may be a werewolf. The second season could use this story to explore the themes of identity, friendship, and trust, as well as to create some suspenseful and scary scenes.

    Cast & Characters

    Goosebumps Season 2 will likely feature the same cast from the first season, as well as some new additions. The main cast of the first season includes:

    • Justin Long is Mr. Bratt, an English teacher who is possessed by Harold Biddle.
    • Jeremy Ray Taylor as Lucas, a smart and curious kid who loves horror stories and is possessed by Harold Biddle at the end of the first season.
    • Madison Iseman as Isabella, a rebellious and adventurous kid who is Lucas’ best friend and crush.
    • Caleel Harris as James, a funny and loyal kid who is Isabella’s brother and Lucas’ friend.
    • Peyton Wich is Isaiah, a sarcastic and skeptical kid who is James’ friend and rival.
    • Shad Moss as Margot, a cool and confident kid who is Isaiah’s cousin and friend.

    The second season may also feature some guest stars and cameo appearances from celebrities or actors who have been involved in previous adaptations of Goosebumps, such as Jack Black, R.L. Stine, or Ryan Gosling. The second season may also introduce some new characters, based on the stories that will be adapted, such as Slappy, the werewolf, or the zombies.

    Where Can I Watch This Series?

    Goosebumps Season 2 will be available to watch on Disney+ and Hulu, as the show is a joint venture between the two streaming platforms. Disney+ is the home of all the Disney-owned content, such as Marvel, Star Wars, Pixar, and more. Hulu is the home of more adult-oriented and original content, such as The Handmaid’s Tale, Castle Rock, and Runaways. Goosebumps is a show that appeals to both kids and adults, and that is why it is available on both platforms.

    To watch Goosebumps Season 2, you will need a subscription to either Disney+ or Hulu, or both. Disney+ costs $7.99 per month or $79.99 per year.
