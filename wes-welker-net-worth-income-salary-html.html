    Originally from Colorado, Wesley Carter “Wes” Welker (born May 1, 1981) is an American football wide receiver and return specialist who now plays for the Denver Broncos of the National Football League’s National Football Conference (NFL). He attended Texas Tech University and was signed by the San Diego Chargers as an undrafted free agent after graduating from collegiate football. Later in his career, he was a member of the Miami Dolphins and the New England Patriots. Welker has had a remarkable career despite the fact that he was not picked. One player in NFL history, Gale Sayers, accumulated more all-purpose yards in his first three seasons in the league than Welker did with the Dolphins. Welker also holds the Dolphins’ all-time records for total kickoff returns, kickoff return yardage, and total punt returns, all of which were set during his first three seasons with the team. Welker also owns the record for the most catches by an undrafted player in NFL history, having surpassed Rod Smith’s previous mark set in 201Welker topped the NFL in catches three times during his time with the Patriots: in 2007, 2009, and 201Welker has the four greatest single-season reception totals in Patriots history, as well as four of the top ten receiving yardage totals, including the team record, and four of the top ten receiving yardage totals in the league. His other franchise marks include the most catches in a single game, the most receiving yards in a single game, the longest reception and the most receptions in a single season. Welker, who has had three straight seasons with at least 110 receptions (and a total of five), is the first wide receiver in NFL history to have at least three seasons with at least 110 receptions, as well as the first to have five seasons with at least 100 receptions. He was named to the Pro Bowl, the All-Pro Team, or both in every season of his Patriots’ career, including the most recent.
