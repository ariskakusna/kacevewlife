    Renowned sportscaster and NASCAR luminary Ken Squier bid farewell to the world on November 15, 2023, at the age of 88, leaving behind a legacy that forever altered the landscape of stock car racing.

    NASCAR’s Voice for Decades

    Born on April 10, 1935, in Waterbury, Vermont, Ken Squier’s journey in NASCAR began in the early 1960s, where he started as a radio personality in Vermont and New Hampshire. His exceptional career at CBS, spanning from 1968 to 1997, positioned him as the iconic voice of NASCAR.

     The Daytona 500 and a Pivotal Moment

    In 1979, Squier took the helm as the commentator for the Daytona 500, delivering the first live flag-to-flag telecast of the race. It was during this historic event that he coined the phrase “The Great American Race,” forever etching his name in the annals of NASCAR history.

    NASCAR Pays Tribute

    Jim France, NASCAR Chairman and CEO, paid heartfelt tribute to Squier, acknowledging his profound impact on the sport. Despite never driving a stock car, Squier’s contributions were likened to those of the racers he passionately covered.

    Legacy in Motorsports Hall of Fame

    Squier’s dedication to motorsports was immortalized with his inductions into the Motorsports Hall of Fame of America in 2010 and the NASCAR Hall of Fame in 201These honors stand as a testament to his enduring influence on the sport.

    Personal Life and Final Days

    While specific details surrounding Squier’s passing remain undisclosed, it’s known that he had entered hospice care, facing health challenges. Beyond the racetrack, Squier’s life was enriched by his marriage to Elizabeth, offering a glimpse into the man behind the microphone.

    A Lasting Impact on NASCAR

    Ken Squier’s impact on NASCAR extended far beyond the racetrack. His insightful commentary during the Daytona 500 and decades of dedicated broadcasting significantly contributed to the sport’s global appeal, making him a beloved figure among fans.

    Fond Farewells and Heartfelt Condolences

    As the racing community mourns the loss of a true legend, our heartfelt condolences go out to Ken Squier’s family and friends. His passion for NASCAR and his gift for captivating audiences through his commentary will be cherished for generations to come.

    FAQs

     How did Ken Squier influence NASCAR?

    Ken Squier played a vital role in boosting NASCAR’s popularity through his engaging commentary, especially during the groundbreaking 1979 Daytona 500. His unique style and keen observations significantly contributed to the sport’s growth and fanbase.

     What honors were bestowed upon Ken Squier?

    Ken Squier was honored with inductions into the Motorsports Hall of Fame of America in 2010 and the NASCAR Hall of Fame in 2018, recognizing his substantial contributions to both motorsports and broadcasting.

    What legacy does Ken Squier leave behind?

    Ken Squier will be remembered for his deep passion for NASCAR and his remarkable ability to connect with audiences through his commentary. His influence on both the sport and the broadcasting industry will be celebrated for years to come.
